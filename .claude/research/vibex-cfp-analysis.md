## Submission Type Analysis

### Experience Report (5 pages incl. references) — STRONG FIT
- Demands "hands-on experiences, lessons learned, reflective insights"
- Our session logs, git history, and beads records are exactly this
- 5-page limit forces crisp positioning
- Achievable in 5 days
- **Risk:** May be perceived as "just a blog post" without theoretical contribution

### Ongoing Research / Vision / Positioning Paper (5 pages incl. references) — STRONGEST FIT
- "Preliminary results, conceptual explorations, methodological reflections, visionary ideas"
- Allows framing mise en place as a *methodology proposal* with case study evidence
- More academic: can propose research questions and future work
- Positions us as defining a new concept, not just reporting an experience
- **Risk:** Must demonstrate enough theoretical grounding for "research" framing

### Full Research Paper (10 pages) — POOR FIT
- Requires "original completed research" — we have a single case study
- Demands formal methodology, literature review, hypothesis testing
- Not achievable in 5 days; material doesn't support this depth yet

## Strategic Positioning

### First-Edition Advantage
- Reviewers are *defining the field* — novel framing wins
- First workshops accept broader submissions to build the field
- Founding papers get cited more (become "foundational")
- Lower rejection rate typical (~40-50% acceptance vs 20-30% established)
- Contrarian takes valued when field is young

### Our Differentiator
- Only submission likely to have *operational rigor* applied to vibe coding
- Only submission with auditable session logs and structured task records
- Only submission connecting AI collaboration to software engineering discipline
- Only submission with a published article + methodology as proof

### Key Framing
"Vibe coding works when it's disciplined. Mise en place is the discipline."
